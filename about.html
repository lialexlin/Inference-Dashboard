How scoring works

Every ticker gets a Business score and a Valuation score. Each is the equal-weighted average of up to 5 components, z-scored against the ticker's layer peers so the comparison is apples-to-apples. Sign convention: positive is good (strong growth or cheap). Missing components are skipped, not penalized; all are clamped to ±3. The two scores plot the ticker on a 2×2 quadrant.

Business — is the company growing?
  1. Revenue YoY growth (CIQ)
  2. Acceleration: YoY − 3y CAGR (CIQ)
  3. EBITDA margin (CIQ)
  4. Forward-EPS revision since last snapshot (manual)
  5. Signal density: capex / lead-time / backlog / shortage mentions, last 90d (signals feed)
Valuation — is it cheap?
  1. Trailing P/E vs ticker's own 8y median (CIQ history)
  2. Forward P/E vs user-supplied 5y median (manual)
  3. Trailing P/E vs layer-peer median (CIQ)
  4. EV/EBITDA vs layer-peer median (CIQ)
  5. 1y price return vs layer-peer median (yfinance)
Quadrants (threshold ±0.3 on each axis)
Label Business Valuation Meaning
Underappreciated > +0.3 > +0.3 Strong fundamentals AND cheap vs. peers — top-idea zone.
Priced in > +0.3 < −0.3 Strong fundamentals but already expensive — consensus trade.
Value trap < −0.3 > +0.3 Cheap on paper but weak fundamentals — optical bargain.
Avoid < −0.3 < −0.3 Weak fundamentals AND expensive — no edge either way.
Fair else else Middle ground — fundamentals and price roughly in balance.
Layer roll-up

A layer takes its players' quadrants and labels the layer using the same vocabulary: Underappreciated if ≥30% of scored players land there (lowest bar — actionable zone); otherwise Priced-in, Value trap, or Avoid if ≥50% cluster in that quadrant; otherwise Fair. A seed-level status_override pins the label when set.
